## Q1. Define Inferential Statistics in your own words with one real-life example.

**Answer:**  
Inferential statistics is the branch of statistics that uses sample data to make predictions or generalizations about a larger population.  

**Example:** If we survey 200 voters to predict the outcome of an election, we are using inferential statistics.

---

## Q2. List the major differences between Descriptive and Inferential Statistics.

| Basis | Descriptive Statistics | Inferential Statistics |
|-------|------------------------|------------------------|
| Purpose | Summarizes data | Makes predictions about population |
| Data | Uses complete data given | Uses sample data |
| Tools | Mean, Median, Charts | Confidence intervals, Hypothesis testing |
| Outcome | Describes what is | Predicts what could be |

---

## Q3. Explain the importance of sample and population in Inferential Statistics.

**Answer:**  
The population is the entire group we want to study, while a sample is a subset of that population. Inferential statistics uses sample data to draw conclusions about the population, saving time and cost.

---

## Q4. Describe one situation where collecting data from the entire population is not possible.

**Answer:**  
Testing the lifespan of light bulbs. It is not practical to test every bulb because testing would destroy them. Instead, a sample is tested.

---

## Q5. What is a confidence interval? Write a short explanation in 2–3 lines.

**Answer:**  
A confidence interval is a range of values used to estimate a population parameter. It gives an interval within which the true value is likely to lie, with a certain level of confidence (e.g., 95%).

---

## Q6. What is meant by hypothesis testing? Why is it used in statistics?

**Answer:**  
Hypothesis testing is a statistical method used to test a claim or assumption about a population parameter using sample data. It helps in decision-making based on evidence.

---

## Q7. Write down any 3 practical examples where hypothesis testing can be used.

**Answer:**  
1. Testing if a new drug is more effective than the old one.  
2. Checking if a machine produces items within quality standards.  
3. Determining whether a marketing campaign increased sales.

---

## Q8. Describe the basic idea behind “testing a claim” using statistics.

**Answer:**  
Testing a claim involves collecting sample data, forming null and alternative hypotheses, calculating a test statistic, and deciding whether there is enough evidence to reject the null hypothesis.

---

## Q9. What is meant by the term significance level (α)? Write its usual values.

**Answer:**  
The significance level (α) is the probability of rejecting the null hypothesis when it is actually true.  
Common values: 0.05, 0.01, and 0.10.

---

## Q10. What is the meaning of p-value in simple terms?

**Answer:**  
The p-value is the probability of observing the sample result, or something more extreme, assuming the null hypothesis is true. A small p-value indicates strong evidence against the null hypothesis.

---

## Q11. Define the Null Hypothesis (H₀) and Alternate Hypothesis (H₁) in one line each.

**Answer:**  
**H₀ (Null Hypothesis):** A statement that there is no effect or no difference.  
**H₁ (Alternate Hypothesis):** A statement that there is an effect or a difference.

---

## Q12. Write Null and Alternate Hypotheses for this case:  
"A company claims their average delivery time is 2 days."

**Answer:**  
**H₀:** μ = 2 days  
**H₁:** μ ≠ 2 days

---

## Q13. In your own words, explain what it means to “reject the null hypothesis.”

**Answer:**  
Rejecting the null hypothesis means there is enough statistical evidence from the sample data to support the alternative hypothesis.

---

## Q14. Give an example of a research question and write both H₀ and H₁.

**Answer:**  
**Research Question:** Does a new teaching method improve student scores?  

**H₀:** The new teaching method does not improve scores.  
**H₁:** The new teaching method improves scores.

---

## Q15. Why do we need both null and alternate hypotheses in a test?

**Answer:**  
We need both because hypothesis testing compares two opposing statements. The null provides a baseline assumption, and the alternative represents the claim being tested.
