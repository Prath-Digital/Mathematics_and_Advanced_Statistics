## Q1. What is the Euclidean distance formula between two points in 2D space?
**Answer:**  
The Euclidean distance between two points $(x_1, y_1)$ and $(x_2, y_2)$ is:  
$$d = \sqrt{(x_2 - x_1)^2 + (y_2 - y_1)^2}$$

---

## Q2. How is the distance between two points calculated in 3D space?
**Answer:**  
For points $(x_1, y_1, z_1)$ and $(x_2, y_2, z_2)$:  
$$d = \sqrt{(x_2 - x_1)^2 + (y_2 - y_1)^2 + (z_2 - z_1)^2}$$

---

## Q3. Define the term "Norm" in the context of vectors.
**Answer:**  
A norm is a function that assigns a length or magnitude to a vector.

---

## Q4. What is the mathematical formula for L1 Norm (Manhattan Norm)?
**Answer:**  
$$||x||_1 = \sum |x_i|$$

---

## Q5. What is the mathematical formula for L2 Norm (Euclidean Norm)?
**Answer:**  
$$||x||_2 = \sqrt{\sum x_i^2}$$

---

## Q6. What is a unit vector, and why is it important?
**Answer:**  
A unit vector has magnitude 1 and represents direction only. It is important for normalization and direction representation.

---

## Q7. How do you convert a given vector into a unit vector?
**Answer:**  
Divide the vector by its magnitude:  
$$\hat{v} = \frac{v}{||v||}$$

---

## Q8. Define the dot product of two vectors.
**Answer:**  
$$a \cdot b = \sum a_i b_i$$

---

## Q9. What geometric meaning does the dot product represent?
**Answer:**  
It represents the cosine of the angle between two vectors:  
$$a \cdot b = ||a|| ||b|| \cos(\theta)$$

---

## Q10. Define the cross product of two vectors.
**Answer:**  
The cross product of two vectors results in a vector perpendicular to both.

---

## Q11. What is the geometric meaning of the cross product in 3D space?
**Answer:**  
Its magnitude represents the area of the parallelogram formed by two vectors.

---

## Q12. Give one real-life example each for the application of dot product and cross product.
**Answer:**  
- Dot Product: Used in calculating work done (force × displacement).  
- Cross Product: Used in torque calculation.
