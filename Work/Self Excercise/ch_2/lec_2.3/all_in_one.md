## Q1. What is a Venn diagram in probability?
A **Venn diagram** is a visual representation used in probability to show all possible outcomes (the sample space) and how different events relate to one another using overlapping shapes, usually circles.

---

## Q2. How are Venn diagrams used to represent events and their relationships?
Venn diagrams represent events as circles inside a rectangle (the sample space).  
- Overlapping regions show common outcomes between events.  
- Non-overlapping regions show outcomes unique to each event.  
This makes relationships like overlap, separation, or containment easy to understand.

---

## Q3. Define union, intersection, and complement of events in the context of Venn diagrams.
- **Union (A ∪ B):** Outcomes that are in event A or event B or both.  
- **Intersection (A ∩ B):** Outcomes common to both events A and B.  
- **Complement (A′):** Outcomes not in event A.

---

## Q4. Give an example of how a Venn diagram can help visualize mutually exclusive events.
If event A = “rolling a 2” and event B = “rolling a 5” on a die, the events are **mutually exclusive**.  
In a Venn diagram, their circles do not overlap, showing they cannot occur together.

---

## Q5. What is a contingency table in probability and statistics?
A **contingency table** is a table used to display the frequency or probability of outcomes for two or more categorical variables at the same time.

---

## Q6. How can a contingency table be used to organize and summarize categorical data?
It organizes data into rows and columns based on categories, allowing:
- Easy comparison between categories  
- Quick calculation of joint, marginal, and conditional probabilities

---

## Q7. Define marginal probability and explain how it is calculated from a contingency table.
**Marginal probability** is the probability of a single event without considering other variables.  
It is calculated by dividing a row or column total by the grand total.

**Formula:**  
P(A) = (Row or Column Total) / (Grand Total)

---

## Q8. What does a marginal probability represent in real-world scenarios?
It represents the overall likelihood of an event occurring regardless of other factors, such as:
- Probability a randomly selected person is female  
- Probability a product is defective overall

---

## Q9. Define joint probability.
**Joint probability** is the probability that two events occur at the same time.

**Notation:**  
P(A ∩ B)

---

## Q10. How is joint probability different from marginal probability?
- **Joint probability:** Probability of two events occurring together  
- **Marginal probability:** Probability of a single event occurring independently of others

---

## Q11. Give an example of calculating joint probability from a contingency table.
If 20 students are both **female and passed**, out of 100 students total:

P(Female ∩ Passed) = 20 / 100 = 0.20

---

## Q12. Why are joint and marginal probabilities important in statistical analysis?
They help:
- Identify relationships between variables  
- Analyze dependencies and patterns in data  
- Build statistical models and make data-driven decisions
