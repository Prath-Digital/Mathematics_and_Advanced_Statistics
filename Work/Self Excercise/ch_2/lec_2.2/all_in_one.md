## Q1. What is a random variable in statistics?

**Answer:**  
A random variable is a numerical function that assigns a real number to each outcome of a random experiment. Its value depends on chance and cannot be predicted with certainty before the experiment is performed.

---

## Q2. Differentiate between a discrete random variable and a continuous random variable.

**Answer:**  

| Discrete Random Variable | Continuous Random Variable |
|--------------------------|----------------------------|
| Takes countable values | Takes uncountable values |
| Values are distinct | Values lie in intervals |
| Uses PMF | Uses PDF |
| Example: number of heads | Example: height of a person |

---

## Q3. Give two real-life examples of random variables.

**Answer:**  
1. Number of customers visiting a store in a day  
2. Time taken to complete an online test  

---

## Q4. What is a probability distribution in the context of random variables?

**Answer:**  
A probability distribution describes how probabilities are assigned to the possible values of a random variable. It fully characterizes the behavior of the random variable.

---

## Q5. Explain the difference between a probability mass function (PMF) and a probability density function (PDF).

**Answer:**  

| PMF | PDF |
|-----|-----|
| Used for discrete variables | Used for continuous variables |
| Assigns probability to exact values | Probability over intervals |
| Sum of probabilities = 1 | Integral over range = 1 |

---

## Q6. What are the essential properties of a probability distribution?

**Answer:**  
1. Each probability is non-negative  
2. Total probability equals 1  
3. Probabilities are defined for all possible values  

---

## Q7. Define the mean (expected value) of a random variable.

**Answer:**  
The mean or expected value is the weighted average of all possible values of a random variable, where weights are their probabilities.

---

## Q8. Why is the mean of a random variable important in probability and statistics?

**Answer:**  
The mean represents the central tendency of a distribution and is used for prediction, comparison, and decision-making.

---

## Q9. Define the variance of a random variable.

**Answer:**  
Variance measures the average squared deviation of values from the mean, indicating how spread out the distribution is.

---

## Q10. How is variance related to the spread or dispersion of a probability distribution?

**Answer:**  
A larger variance means greater dispersion of values around the mean, while a smaller variance indicates values are closely clustered.

---

## Q11. What is the relationship between variance and standard deviation for a random variable?

**Answer:**  
Standard deviation is the square root of variance. Both measure dispersion, but standard deviation is in the same units as the data.

---

## Q12. How can the mean and variance of a random variable be used in decision-making?

**Answer:**  
- Mean helps estimate expected outcomes  
- Variance helps assess risk and uncertainty  
- Together, they support informed and balanced decisions
