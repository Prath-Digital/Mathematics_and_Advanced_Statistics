# Self Exercise - 5.1

## Q1. What is Linear Algebra, and why is it important in data science and machine learning?
**Answer:**  
Linear Algebra is a branch of mathematics that deals with vectors, matrices, and linear equations.  
It is important because it forms the foundation of machine learning algorithms, data representation, transformations, and optimizations.

---

## Q2. Define a point in mathematics with an example.
**Answer:**  
A point represents a location in space and has no size or dimension.  
Example: (3, 4) represents a point in 2D space.

---

## Q3. How can a point be represented in 2D and 3D space?
**Answer:**  
- In 2D: (x, y)  
- In 3D: (x, y, z)

---

## Q4. Define a vector. How does it differ from a point?
**Answer:**  
A vector is a quantity that has both magnitude and direction.  
A point represents a position, while a vector represents movement or direction.

---

## Q5. Explain the components of a vector with an example in 2D.
**Answer:**  
A vector in 2D has two components: (x, y).  
Example: v = (2, 3) means 2 units in x-direction and 3 units in y-direction.

---

## Q6. What are the basic operations that can be performed on vectors?
**Answer:**  
- Addition  
- Subtraction  
- Scalar multiplication  
- Dot product  
- Cross product (only defined for 3D vectors)

---

## Q7. Explain vector addition with a simple example.
**Answer:**  
Add corresponding components:  
(2, 3) + (1, 4) = (3, 7)

---

## Q8. Explain vector subtraction with an example.
**Answer:**  
Subtract corresponding components:  
(5, 6) - (2, 3) = (3, 3)

---

## Q9. What is scalar multiplication of a vector?
**Answer:**  
Multiplying a vector by a scalar scales its magnitude:  
2 × (3, 4) = (6, 8)

---

## Q10. How does the magnitude (length) of a vector get calculated?
**Answer:**  
For a vector $v = (x, y)$:  
$$
||v|| = \sqrt{x^2 + y^2}
$$
---

## Q11. Why are vectors important in machine learning?
**Answer:**  
Vectors are used to represent data (features), perform computations, and enable algorithms like regression, classification, and neural networks.

---

## Q12. Give one real-life example where vectors are used to represent data.
**Answer:**  
In recommendation systems (like Netflix or YouTube), user preferences are represented as vectors to suggest relevant content.
