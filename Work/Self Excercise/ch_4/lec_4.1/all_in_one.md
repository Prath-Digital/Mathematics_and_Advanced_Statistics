## Q1. What is meant by a statistical distribution?

**Answer:**\
A statistical distribution describes how values of a variable are spread
or distributed. It shows the possible values the variable can take and
how frequently (or with what probability) those values occur.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Q2. What is meant by a statistical distribution?

**Answer:**\
A statistical distribution is a mathematical function that provides
probabilities of occurrence of different possible outcomes for a random
variable.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Q3. What is a Q-Q Plot?

**Answer:**\
A Q-Q (Quantile-Quantile) Plot is a graphical tool used to compare the
quantiles of a dataset with the quantiles of a theoretical distribution
(commonly the normal distribution).

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Q4. How does a Q-Q plot help in checking normality of data?

**Answer:**\
- If the plotted points lie approximately on a straight diagonal line,
the data is likely normally distributed.\
- If the points deviate significantly from the line, the data may not be
normally distributed.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Q5. Differentiate between discrete and continuous distributions with examples.

**Answer:**

  Feature       Discrete Distribution        Continuous Distribution
  ------------- ---------------------------- ---------------------------
  Values        Countable values             Infinite possible values
  Example       Number of students           Height of students
  Probability   Probability at exact value   Probability over interval

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Q6. Give two real-world examples of discrete probability distributions.

**Answer:**\
1. Number of heads in 10 coin tosses.\
2. Number of defective items in a batch.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Q7. Give two real-world examples of continuous probability distributions.

**Answer:**\
1. Height of individuals.\
2. Time taken to complete a task.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Q8. What is a Bernoulli distribution?

**Answer:**\
A Bernoulli distribution is a discrete probability distribution for a
random variable that has only two possible outcomes:\
- Success (1)\
- Failure (0)

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Q9. State the conditions under which a random variable follows a Bernoulli distribution.

**Answer:**\
1. There are only two outcomes (success/failure).\
2. Each trial is independent.\
3. The probability of success (p) remains constant.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Q10. What is a Binomial distribution?

**Answer:**\
A Binomial distribution models the number of successes in a fixed number
of independent Bernoulli trials.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Q11. How is the binomial distribution related to repeated Bernoulli trials?

**Answer:**\
A binomial distribution represents the total number of successes
obtained after repeating a Bernoulli experiment multiple times under
identical conditions.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Q12. Mention two practical applications of the binomial distribution.

**Answer:**\
1. Quality control (counting defective items in a sample).\
2. Survey analysis (counting number of people who respond "Yes").
