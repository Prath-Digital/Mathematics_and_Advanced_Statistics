## Q1. What is Statistics, and how is it useful in real-world applications?

**Answer:**  
Statistics is the science of **collecting, organizing, analyzing, interpreting, and presenting data** to make informed decisions.

**Real-world usefulness:**
- Helps governments plan policies using census data
- Enables businesses to analyze sales and customer behavior
- Assists doctors in evaluating treatment effectiveness
- Supports researchers in validating hypotheses

---

## Q2. How is Statistics applied in Data Science?

**Answer:**  
Statistics forms the backbone of Data Science by enabling data-driven reasoning.

**Applications include:**
- Data cleaning and summarization
- Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)
- Probability-based modeling
- Hypothesis testing
- Building and validating machine learning models

**Answer:**  
| Data | Information |
|-----|-------------|

## Q5. What are the two main types of data in Statistics?

**Answer:**
1. **Numerical (Quantitative) Data**
2. **Categorical (Qualitative) Data**

---

## Q6. Differentiate between Numerical Data and Categorical Data with examples.

**Answer:**  
| Numerical Data | Categorical Data |
|---------------|-----------------|
| Measurable values | Descriptive labels |
| Can perform arithmetic | Cannot perform arithmetic |
| Example: Height, Age | Example: Gender, Blood Group |

---

## Q7. What is the difference between Discrete and Continuous numerical data?

**Answer:**  
| Discrete Data | Continuous Data |
|--------------|----------------|
| Countable values | Measurable values |
| Whole numbers | Any value in a range |
| Example: Number of students | Example: Weight, Temperature |

## Q9. Explain the use of bar graphs, line charts, and histograms in data representation.

**Answer:**
- **Bar Graphs:** Compare categories
- **Line Charts:** Show trends over time
- **Histograms:** Display data distribution across intervals

---

## Q10. How can pie charts and box plots help in understanding the distribution of data?

**Answer:**
- **Pie Charts:** Show proportion or percentage contribution of categories
- **Box Plots:** Display median, quartiles, spread, and outliers

---

## Q11. What is the difference between Population and Sample in statistics?

**Answer:**  
| Population | Sample |
|-----------|--------|
| Entire group | Subset of population |
| More accurate | Less costly and faster |
| Example: All voters | Example: 1,000 voters surveyed |

---

## Q12. Why do data scientists often work with samples instead of entire populations?

**Answer:**
- Populations are often too large
- Saves time and cost
- Easier data collection
- Statistical methods allow accurate inference from samples
