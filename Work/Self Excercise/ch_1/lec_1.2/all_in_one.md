## Q1. What are the two main types of Statistics?
**Answer:**  
The two main types of Statistics are:
1. **Descriptive Statistics**
2. **Inferential Statistics**

---

## Q2. Differentiate between Descriptive Statistics and Inferential Statistics.
**Answer:**

| Descriptive Statistics | Inferential Statistics |
|------------------------|------------------------|
| Summarizes data | Draws conclusions from data |
| Uses entire dataset | Uses sample data |
| No predictions | Makes predictions or generalizations |
| Example: Mean, charts | Example: Hypothesis testing |

---

## Q3. Give one practical example of each type of statistics.
**Answer:**
- **Descriptive Statistics:** Calculating the average marks of students in a class.
- **Inferential Statistics:** Predicting the average marks of all students in a school based on a sample.

---

## Q4. What is Descriptive Statistics? Why is it important in data analysis?
**Answer:**  
Descriptive Statistics is the branch of statistics that summarizes and organizes data using numerical measures and visual tools.  
It is important because it:
- Simplifies large datasets
- Helps identify patterns and trends
- Makes data easy to understand

---

## Q5. What kind of information can we derive using Descriptive Statistics?
**Answer:**  
We can derive:
- Central tendency (mean, median, mode)
- Data spread (range, variance, standard deviation)
- Distribution shape
- Minimum and maximum values

---

## Q6. List some tools or techniques used in descriptive statistics.
**Answer:**  
- Mean
- Median
- Mode
- Range
- Variance
- Standard Deviation
- Tables
- Bar charts
- Histograms
- Pie charts

---

## Q7. Define Mean. How is it calculated?
**Answer:**  
The **Mean** is the average of a set of values.  
It is calculated by dividing the sum of all values by the total number of values.

**Formula:**  
Mean = (Sum of all values) / (Number of values)

---

## Q8. What is the Median, and when is it preferred over the Mean?
**Answer:**  
The **Median** is the middle value when data is arranged in ascending or descending order.  
It is preferred over the mean when the data contains **extreme values (outliers)**.

---

## Q9. What is Mode, and in what type of data is it most useful?
**Answer:**  
The **Mode** is the value that occurs most frequently in a dataset.  
It is most useful for:
- Categorical data
- Non-numerical data (e.g., colors, brands)

---

## Q10. What is Range, and what does it represent?
**Answer:**  
The **Range** is the difference between the highest and lowest values in a dataset.  
It represents the **spread of the data**.

**Formula:**  
Range = Maximum value − Minimum value

---

## Q11. Define Variance and explain its significance.
**Answer:**  
**Variance** measures how far data points are spread from the mean.  
It is significant because it shows the level of variability in the dataset.

---

## Q12. How is Standard Deviation calculated, and why is it important in understanding data spread?
**Answer:**  
**Standard Deviation** is the square root of variance.

**Steps to calculate:**
1. Find the mean
2. Subtract mean from each value
3. Square the differences
4. Find the average of squared differences
5. Take the square root

**Importance:**
- Shows how close data points are to the mean
- Helps compare data variability
- Widely used in data analysis and research
